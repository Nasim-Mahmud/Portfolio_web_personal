Govt. Laboratory High School, Rajshahi

2005 - 2012

Some of the best years of my life were spent in school. I spent seven years at my school, where I was admitted into third grade. I won't lie; I didn't anticipate missing school so much these days. It was a time of academic and personal growth and learning. I was thrilled to be admitted to our school because it was one of the finest institutions in the division. I created a great deal of memories and friends. I still remember feeling nervous and thrilled on my first day of school. I also recall my final day of school when I mixed feelings of sadness at leaving and excitement at beginning the next phase of my life.

The teachers were amazing. They were kind, patient, and supportive. They challenged us academically, but they also helped us to grow and develop as individuals. I am grateful for the teachers who made a difference in my life. We raised to new classes each year. Throughout my years in school, I made a lot of friends. We were in class, lunch, and extracurricular activities together for countless hours. We experienced joy, sorrow, and support through good times and bad.

I participated in many extracurricular activities during my school days, including sports competitions, the debating club, and the Boys Scouts. These activities helped me to develop my skills. The class ten school picnic is something I will always remember. I had great fun on my first-ever picnic, which was awesome. We had delicious food, participated in games, and sang songs. It was a day I am going to cherish forever. We were all thrilled to be the most senior students in the entire school when we were in class ten. We were unaware that it marked the conclusion of our prolonged journey together.

Graduation was a bittersweet day. We were excited to start the next chapter of our lives but were also sad to be leaving each other behind. I am grateful for the memories I made with my friends during school. They are some of the most precious memories of my life.
